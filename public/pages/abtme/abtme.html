  • How did you learn to draw?

    I don't know if I can give much of a helpful answer to this question, because truth be told, as far as I can remember I've always liked drawing and I've always been drawing.

    One thing that I like doing is picking an artist who's art style I really enjoy and dissecting it. Try to figure out the steps they took to achieve that look in their work and try to replicate it. Incorporate what you learned in your own art style. Two artists with awesome art styles you can look at are Jewellier and J. C. Leyendecker (this site has a great collection of his work)!

    I also like doing value and anatomy exercises, I find that values is something beginner artists tend to ignore a lot, and it's a simple concept that can take your art from a 60% to 100%. I mostly enjoy painting, and I feel like it's the thing that really stands out in my work, so It's what I focus on. The very basis of painting is value and color, so if that's something you wanna learn, focus on those two first - textures, brushstroke coordination etc. aren't gonna mean much if your colors are muddy and all the greys in your drawing blend together.

    One book I can recommend and that I reference very often for my work is The Art of Animal Drawing, Construction, Action Analysis, Caricature (Dover Art Instruction) by Ken Hultgren - It gives a great breakdown of the anatomy of various types of animals and common ways to stylize them.
